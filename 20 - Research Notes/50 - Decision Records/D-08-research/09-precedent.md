# Precedent Survey For DSE Latency Sources

## Precedent Archetypes

The named DSL/HLS/RTL projects suggest five repeatable latency-source archetypes, not one universal answer (unverified). First is the **analytic estimator**: LegUp-, Bambu-, Calyx-, Dahlia-, Halide-, and HeteroCL-like flows tend to keep a compiler-side schedule/cost model so the search can rank points before expensive backend runs (unverified). Second is **report-guided DSE**: HLS-centric flows, including hls4ml-, FINN-, and AutoDSE-like workflows, often run synthesis for selected configurations and use tool reports as the latency/resource scoreboard (unverified). Third is **simulation-guided validation**: C/RTL simulation or co-simulation checks concrete workloads after a point is chosen or sampled, but is usually too slow to be the inner-loop oracle (unverified). Fourth is a **surrogate/cached estimator**: Bayesian, random-forest, or memoized DSE learns from prior HLS runs and avoids re-synthesizing equivalent points (unverified). Fifth is **hybrid calibration**: a fast estimator proposes and prunes, reports calibrate or override, and simulation validates suspicious winners (unverified).

## Spatial Baseline

Spatial's current DSE contract is closest to an analytic estimator with batch execution. `DSEAnalyzer` builds the search space, calls `compileLatencyModel()`, then dispatches heuristic, brute force, experiment, or HyperMapper modes (`src/spatial/dse/DSEAnalyzer.scala:59-80`). Compilation requires `model/model_dse.scala`, locks `model_dse.scala.lock`, and shells out to `bash scripts/assemble.sh` (`src/spatial/dse/DSEAnalyzer.scala:90-147`). `LatencyAnalyzer.test` then locates a `RuntimeModel-assembly` jar, batches rewrite parameters in groups of 1000, runs `java -jar ... ni tune ...`, parses `"Total Cycles for App"`, and stores the parsed totals (`src/spatial/dse/LatencyAnalyzer.scala:29-47`). The DSE CSV header treats that value as the `Cycles` column beside area and validity (`src/spatial/dse/DSEAnalyzer.scala:354-360`). The DSE spec summarizes this as an out-of-process compiled model rather than an in-memory analyzer (`10 - Spec/70 - Models and DSE/40 - Design Space Exploration.md:52`).

## Runtime-Model Contract

The generated model is not backend-neutral measurement; it is Spatial schedule metadata re-expressed as Scala equations. `RuntimeModelGenerator` emits `AppRuntimeModel_<version>`, recognizes noninteractive and repeated `tune` groups, sets `tuneParams`, executes the root controller, and prints the total-cycle line consumed by DSE (`src/spatial/model/RuntimeModelGenerator.scala:111-153`). For controller nodes it repeatedly derives `lat` from `lhs.bodyLatency.sum` for inner controls and `ii` from `lhs.II`, then constructs `ControllerModel`s with schedule, cchain, latency, and II (`src/spatial/model/RuntimeModelGenerator.scala:261-344`). Dense transfers are emitted as `DenseLoad` or `DenseStore` controller models with cchains and bit widths, not as parsed HLS loop reports (`src/spatial/model/RuntimeModelGenerator.scala:348-370`). In `RuntimeModel.scala`, dense transfers call `congestionModel`, which uses fitted parameters, competitors, burst alignment, and cchain-derived terms (`models/src/models/RuntimeModel.scala:244-277`). General controller latency is computed by explicit schedule equations for outer and inner controls, then DFS execution multiplies by iteration counts and `totalCycles()` returns `num_cycles` (`models/src/models/RuntimeModel.scala:313-347`, `models/src/models/RuntimeModel.scala:404`).

## Mapping The Archetypes

Analytic-estimator precedent maps cleanly to current Spatial parity: it is cheap, batchable, and structurally tied to compiler IR. Report-guided precedent does not map to the current hot loop because the active path compiles a Scala jar, not an HLS project, and parses one text line rather than accepted loop II, loop latency, or top-kernel report fields (`src/spatial/dse/LatencyAnalyzer.scala:35-46`). Simulation-guided validation fits poorly as a primary source for the same reason: current Spatial can score 1000 tuned points per jar invocation, whereas RTL/co-sim would be point- and testbench-dependent (unverified). Surrogate/cached precedent fits the existing CSV/HyperMapper shape: DSE already writes `Cycles`/`Valid` rows and sorts/analyzes the resulting CSV (`src/spatial/dse/DSEAnalyzer.scala:420-438`), while the spec says HyperMapper mode uses objectives `Slices` and `Cycles` plus feasible output `Valid` (`10 - Spec/70 - Models and DSE/40 - Design Space Exploration.md:54`). Hybrid calibration also matches the spec's HLS note: the architecture is reusable, but HLS cycle estimation should not depend on the old generated Scala runtime jar (`10 - Spec/70 - Models and DSE/40 - Design Space Exploration.md:62-64`).

## D-08 Implication

Precedent argues against choosing pure HLS reports/simulation as the only DSE latency source: it is high fidelity after synthesis, but too expensive and too late for broad search (unverified). It also argues against unmodified Spatial runtime-model parity as the final HLS answer, because the local model is explicitly driven by Spatial `bodyLatency`, `II`, schedule equations, and fitted transfer congestion, not backend-accepted HLS scheduling. The best D-08 answer is therefore a **hybrid calibrated estimator**: keep runtime-model parity only as the v0/pre-HLS fallback, introduce a new HLS-aware estimator for search-time latency, cache/surrogate expensive observations, and let HLS synthesis reports override or calibrate the estimator once available. Simulation should validate representative winners, regressions, and report/parser doubts, not define every candidate's score. In short: new estimator for the search loop, report-guided calibration for authority, simulation-guided validation for confidence.
