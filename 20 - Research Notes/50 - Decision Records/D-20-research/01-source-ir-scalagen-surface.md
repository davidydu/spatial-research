# DelayLine Source, IR, and ScalaGen Surface

## Decision Frame

[[D-20]] asks whether Rust simulation should be value-only and elide `DelayLine`, or cycle-aware and model retiming registers. The decision queue points directly at `ScalaGenDelays` and `RetimingTransformer`, and frames the choice as "Rust simulation is value-only and elides `DelayLine`, or is cycle-aware and models retiming registers" (`20 - Research Notes/40 - Decision Queue.md:87`, `20 - Research Notes/40 - Decision Queue.md:89`). The related [[C0 - Retiming]] spec is marked Chisel-specific, and says retiming computes `fullDelay`, inserts `DelayLine(size, data)` nodes, and collapses redundant `RetimeGate()` markers (`10 - Spec/40 - Compiler Passes/C0 - Retiming.md:19`, `10 - Spec/40 - Compiler Passes/C0 - Retiming.md:28`, `10 - Spec/40 - Compiler Passes/C0 - Retiming.md:30`).

## User and IR Creation

The user-facing API is `retime(delay, payload)` in `MiscAPI`. Negative delays throw, delay zero returns `payload.unbox`, and positive delays stage `DelayLine(delay, payload)` while marking the resulting symbol `userInjectedDelay = true` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/lang/api/MiscAPI.scala:23`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/lang/api/MiscAPI.scala:30`). `retimeGate()` separately stages `RetimeGate()` as a barrier (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/lang/api/MiscAPI.scala:19`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/lang/api/MiscAPI.scala:20`).

The IR node itself is tiny: `DelayLine[A:Bits](size: Int, data: Bits[A]) extends Primitive[A]`, with only one rewrite, `size == 0` folds back to `data.unbox` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/node/DelayLine.scala:8`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/node/DelayLine.scala:9`). The [[50 - Primitives]] spec records the same contract: `DelayLine` is a synthetic register chain used to balance pipeline depths, with `size` as cycle count, while `RetimeGate` is a `Simple` effect scheduling barrier (`10 - Spec/30 - IR/10 - Spatial Nodes/50 - Primitives.md:123`, `10 - Spec/30 - IR/10 - Spatial Nodes/50 - Primitives.md:127`).

## Retiming Insertion and Registration

Compiler-inserted lines come from `RetimingTransformer`. `findDelayLines` delegates to `computeDelayLines` with a `createLine` closure that calls `delayLine(size, f(data))` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:32`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:38`). `delayLine` stages `DelayLine(size, data.view[Bits])` and names it from the producer (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:68`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:72`). `computeDelayLines` computes each reader's critical path, compares each bit input's achieved and missing latency, and emits a `ValueDelay` when the integer delay is nonzero (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/util/modeling.scala:618`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/util/modeling.scala:634`).

The transformer does not immediately replace every use. `addDelayLine` stores each `ValueDelay` in both `delayLines` by input and `delayConsumers` by reader (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:90`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:98`). During `retimeStm`, `registerDelays` calls `line.value()` and registers `in -> dly` before visiting the reader, then registers the old reader to the mirrored reader (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:78`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:87`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:142`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/transform/RetimingTransformer.scala:152`). `ValueDelay.value()` memoizes the staged symbol, so shared or extended delay chains are materialized once (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/metadata/retiming/ValueDelay.scala:13`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/metadata/retiming/ValueDelay.scala:18`; [[40 - Retiming]]). The `SimpleRetimePipe` test asserts this is observable in IR by expecting three `DelayLine` nodes for `d := a * b + c` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/test/spatial/tests/feature/retiming/SimpleRetimePipe.scala:21`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/test/spatial/tests/feature/retiming/SimpleRetimePipe.scala:23`).

## ScalaGen, ChiselGen, and Constants

`ScalaGenSpatial` mixes in `ScalaGenDelays` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenSpatial.scala:28`). That trait erases the timing effect: constant delay lines emit nothing, and non-constant delay lines emit only `val lhs = data` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenDelays.scala:10`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenDelays.scala:12`; [[60 - Counters and Primitives]]). Constant naming is handled in `NamedCodegen`, which quotes a constant `DelayLine` as the underlying data (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/naming/NamedCodegen.scala:101`). Chisel has the same constant-alias naming rule (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselCodegen.scala:42`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselCodegen.scala:44`), but `ChiselGen` also mixes in `ChiselGenDelay` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGen.scala:5`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGen.scala:10`).

For hardware, `ChiselGenDelay` skips broadcast-address, constant, and parameter data, but otherwise bumps `maxretime`, emits a `Wire`, and assigns through `DL(data.r, delay, false)` or vector element variants (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenDelay.scala:17`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenDelay.scala:30`). `DL` lowers bits to `.DS(...)` and words to `getRetimed(...)`, gated by backpressure and sometimes forward pressure (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenCommon.scala:288`, `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenCommon.scala:293`; [[60 - Math and Primitives]]). This is the split D-20 must name: ScalaGen treats `DelayLine` as a value alias, while ChiselGen treats it as concrete retiming state.
