# D-07 Angle 3: `MemoryConfigurer` Contract

## 1. Orchestration Scope

`MemoryConfigurer.configure` is a whole-memory analysis pass, not a local partition-factor picker. It logs the memory, clears stale `Dispatch`, `Ports`, and `Duplicates` metadata, extracts affine access matrices from current readers and writers, invokes banking, summarizes instances, finalizes metadata, and then runs a PIR legality check (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:55`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:71`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:78`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:80`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:81`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:82`). Its search space is shaped before grouping: `bankViews` chooses flat versus hierarchical banking from strategy, explicit banking, rank, line buffers, and user flags; `nStricts` and `aStricts` choose best-guess, relaxed, or user-defined constraints; `dimensionDuplication` controls regrouping/fission eligibility (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:25`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:34`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:38`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:42`). An HLS-oriented replacement can restrict the candidate forms to HLS partitions (unverified), but it still has to enter and leave this orchestration boundary with equivalent access and metadata semantics.

## 2. Grouping, Concurrency, and Conflicts

Read and write sets are first grouped unless forced explicit banking bypasses grouping (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:440`). The default grouping rule is two-dimensional: an access joins a group only when some existing member needs a concurrent port with it, and all such concurrent members are non-conflicting or broadcastable (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:222`). Concurrency is derived from control structure, unroll identity, inner pipes, fully unrolled inner seqs, parallel controllers, outer pipelines without intervening writes, delay alignment, and stream control (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:418`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:427`). Conflict means same-port, overlapping address, not broadcastable, same segment assignment, and not exempted by lock SRAM/conflictable flags (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:252`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:258`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:267`). Broadcast is itself constrained to arg-in or identical accesses, otherwise same read node and matrix, no writes, and lockstep or provably diverged iterators under broadcast enablement (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:195`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:198`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:200`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:202`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:212`). The optional unroll-first grouping path preserves the same `samePort && !conflict` invariant, with per-access grouping then cross-access merging (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:343`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:352`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:384`).

## 3. Banking, Buffering, and Cost

`bankGroups` filters writes to those reaching the reads unless unused accesses are kept, enumerates combinations of view, `N`, alpha, and regrouping directives, sorts and filters them, then calls the selected `BankingStrategy.bankAccesses` with buffer depth included (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:611`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:616`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:619`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:631`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:633`). `computeMemoryBufferPorts` computes the metapipeline controller, per-access `Option[Int]` buffer port, and possible hierarchical or write-buffer hazard issue; non-buffer memories force `Some(0)`, while metapipe distances are normalized by the minimum distance (`src/spatial/metadata/control/package.scala:1280`, `src/spatial/metadata/control/package.scala:1292`, `src/spatial/metadata/control/package.scala:1295`, `src/spatial/metadata/control/package.scala:1300`, `src/spatial/metadata/control/package.scala:1317`, `src/spatial/metadata/control/package.scala:1319`). Cost selection then builds read/write mux-width histograms, estimates normalized LUT, FF, BRAM, and SRAM auxiliary arithmetic costs, sums costs across duplicates, and chooses the lowest total scheme and option (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:448`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:451`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:455`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:467`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:646`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:653`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:659`).

## 4. Ports and Duplicate Instances

Winning banking creates `Instance` objects containing grouped reads/writes, controllers, metapipe, banking, depth, cost, per-access ports, padding, and accumulator type (`src/spatial/metadata/memory/BankingData.scala:109`). Padding is derived from selected `P` values, ports are recomputed from winning read groups and reaching write groups, and same-controller read/write pairs promote `AccumType.Buff` (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:663`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:664`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:665`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:668`). `computePorts` groups by buffer port, assigns one `muxPort` per banking group, assigns `muxOfs` in unroll order, and builds broadcast/cast groups where later receivers reuse the first sender's offset (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:542`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:545`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:552`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:581`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:586`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:588`). The `Port` fields are the downstream contract: `bufferPort`, `muxPort`, `muxOfs`, `castgroup`, and `broadcast` (`src/spatial/metadata/memory/BankingData.scala:99`).

## 5. Final Metadata the Planner Must Preserve

`finalize` converts each `Instance` to a physical `Memory` duplicate, installs `mem.duplicates`, annotates each access uid with `GroupId`, `Ports`, and `Dispatch`, warns on read-before-write, and marks unused accesses when allowed (`src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:115`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:116`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:123`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:124`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:125`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:138`, `src/spatial/traversal/banking/MemoryConfigurer.scala:148`). `Duplicates` is the pre-unrolling physical duplicate list; `Dispatch` maps each unrolled uid to duplicate IDs, with writers allowed multiple dispatches and readers expected to have at most one; `GroupId` records banking-analysis group membership; `Ports` maps dispatch id to uid to `Port` (`src/spatial/metadata/memory/BankingData.scala:261`, `src/spatial/metadata/memory/BankingData.scala:289`, `src/spatial/metadata/memory/BankingData.scala:293`, `src/spatial/metadata/memory/BankingData.scala:306`, `src/spatial/metadata/memory/BankingData.scala:312`, `src/spatial/metadata/memory/package.scala:213`, `src/spatial/metadata/memory/package.scala:225`, `src/spatial/metadata/memory/package.scala:234`). Therefore, a Rust/HLS planner must preserve access grouping legality, reaching-write filtering, buffering depth and hazards, mux and broadcast port layout, duplicate/dispatch identity, padding, accumulator flags, merge rejection rules, and issue behavior, not just choose partition factors.
