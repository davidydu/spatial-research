## Baseline

Current Spatial behavior is rejection, not partial lowering. `SpatialVirtualization` reports hard diagnostics for source `return`, `while`, and `do while` in staged applications (`src/spatial/lang/api/SpatialVirtualization.scala:103-114`). This is deliberate because Forge's default virtualized controls would otherwise re-emit native Scala `return`, `while`, and `do while` when a DSL does not override them (`forge/src/forge/EmbeddedControls.scala:48-52`, `forge/src/forge/EmbeddedControls.scala:131-144`). The supported control vocabulary is explicit: counted loops become `OpForeach` over a `CounterChain` (`src/spatial/lang/control/ForeachClass.scala:26-33`, `src/spatial/node/Control.scala:45-54`), and data-dependent loops are expressed as `FSM` / `StateMachine` with `notDone`, `action`, and `nextState` blocks (`src/spatial/lang/control/FSM.scala:18-24`, `src/spatial/node/Control.scala:103-118`).

## Decision Matrix

| Option | Implementation cost | Verification burden | Source compatibility | HLS friendliness | Silent divergence risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Preserve exact restriction | Lowest: detect `while`, `do while`, and early `return` in HLS-visible regions, emit diagnostics, and stop before HLS. | Low: parser/HIR negative tests plus checks that no HLS is emitted. | Best compatibility with existing Spatial behavior; worse for Rust users expecting host-language control flow (unverified). | Best: avoids unscheduled loops and hidden exits. | Lowest: unsupported constructs fail loudly. |
| Support only bounded/structured `while` | Medium: require a syntactic or inferred max tripcount and structured state, then lower to a `Foreach`-like loop. Spatial counters already carry start, end, step, and parallelization (`src/spatial/lang/Counter.scala:15-23`). | Medium-high: bound inference, zero-iteration behavior, mutation, and II/resource tests. Existing analysis asks for static loop bounds or explicit II when iteration differences are unknown (`src/spatial/traversal/IterationDiffAnalyzer.scala:37-53`). | Partial: accepts common counted loops, but no longer exactly matches current Spatial rejection. | Good only when bounds are explicit; HLS tools generally prefer known tripcounts for reports and pipelining (unverified). | Medium: inferred bounds or reordered side effects can look plausible but differ from user intent. |
| Support FSM-lowered `while` | High: convert loop-carried variables, memory effects, and condition evaluation into explicit state. | High: simulator/HLS parity, nested controls, stalls, and termination proofs. Chisel codegen already lowers `StateMachine` by wiring state, next-state, and `~notDone` as done (`src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenController.scala:469-485`). | Better for data-dependent loops, but it is new Spatial semantics. | Moderate: explicit FSMs are hardware-shaped, though often less pipeline-friendly than counted loops (unverified). | Medium-high: small differences in state update timing or side-effect commit order may be hard to notice. |
| Support early `return` | High: define value returns, void returns, nested-scope unwinding, and side-effect commit points. | Very high: every consumer must agree on when outputs, streams, memories, and controller children are valid. Existing `breakWhen` is controller-local, resets through controller codegen, and even warns that Scala break timing differs from synthesis timing (`src/spatial/lang/control/Control.scala:41-56`, `src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenController.scala:312-315`, `src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenController.scala:75-93`). | Useful for Rust/C-like authoring (unverified), but incompatible with Spatial's current unsupported `return`. | Mixed: native C++ `return` is not the same as accelerator completion or cleanup (unverified). | High: skipped effects or partially committed outputs are easy to miss in small tests. |
| Full C-like semantics | Very high: combines unbounded loops, early exits, likely `break` / `continue`, and host-like block control. | Maximal: requires a formal control/effect model, equivalence tests, and backend parity across simulation and HLS. | Highest for C/Rust intuition (unverified), lowest for Spatial source compatibility. | Weak unless heavily restricted by pragmas, tripcount assertions, and coding-style rules (unverified). | Highest: generated HLS may compile while meaning something different from Spatial or Rust expectations. |

## Cost Drivers

The hard part is not parsing `while`; it is making control effects explicit enough for Spatial's analysis model. Argon `VarRead` rewrites to the most recent write in the current scope and explicitly assumes all statements in a block execute (`argon/src/argon/node/Var.scala:23-35`). General `while` violates that assumption by executing a body zero or many times; early `return` violates it by skipping later statements. This pushes any support option toward SSA-like loop-carried values, dominance/effect analysis, and backend-specific commit rules (unverified). The more the Rust/HLS frontend accepts host-language control directly, the more it must replace Spatial's current explicit controller discipline with a new one.

## Recommendation

Recommend preserving the exact restriction for D-03. It is the only option that is source-compatible with current Spatial, lowest cost, lowest verification burden, and least likely to silently diverge. If the project wants a future extension, bounded/structured `while` is the smallest next step, but it should be specified as explicit sugar for Spatial-style controllers with mandatory bounds and diagnostics. FSM-lowered `while`, early `return`, and full C-like semantics should remain out of scope until the Rust/HLS frontend has a written control/effect model and parity tests.
