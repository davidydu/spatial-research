# D-22 Angle 6: Risk and Failure Modes

## Elastic Scalagen Compatibility

Elastic Scalagen-compatible simulation is the lowest test-breakage path and the highest hardware-truth risk. The primary ScalaGen path remaps `FIFO[A]` and `StreamIn/StreamOut[A]` to mutable queues, and `LIFO[A]` to a mutable stack (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenFIFO.scala:12-18`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenLIFO.scala:12-18`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenStream.scala:12-16`). Status readers can say full, but enqueue/write still calls `enqueue` or `push` whenever enabled (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenFIFO.scala:19-44`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenLIFO.scala:19-44`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenStream.scala:84-97`). This avoids false failures in old goldens such as value-only FIFO load checks (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/test/spatial/tests/feature/memories/fifo/FIFOLoad.scala:21-53`), but it silently converts overflow into larger host memory. Underflow is also value-masked: ScalaGen returns `invalid` on empty FIFO/stream reads, so a liveness bug may appear only as a later data mismatch (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenFIFO.scala:33-39`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/scalagen/ScalaGenStream.scala:84-90`). The existing spec calls this "silent elastic enqueue" and warns that synthesis can fail where Scalagen passes ([[40 - FIFO LIFO Stream Simulation]]:140-149).

## Bounded Back-pressure Simulation

Bounded back-pressure is the better semantic model for HLS-facing liveness, but it is also where silent deadlocks and scheduler nondeterminism enter. Chisel/Fringe advances controllers only through pressure-qualified signals: `getForwardPressure` checks stream valid/FIFO non-empty, `getBackPressure` checks stream ready/FIFO non-full, and generated kernels wire those predicates into `forwardpressure` and `backpressure` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenCommon.scala:249-278`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/codegen/chiselgen/ChiselGenController.scala:304-309`). `ForkJoin` children synchronize on all `iterDone` bits and increment only under `io.backpressure` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/fringe/src/fringe/templates/Controllers.scala:141-153`), while kernel counters also advance through `forwardpressure` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/fringe/src/fringe/SpatialBlocks.scala:132-158`).

The newer Scala executor shows both promise and maintenance risk. `ScalaQueue` has capacity and throws on full/empty operations (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/executor/scala/memories/ScalaQueue.scala:8-24`), and `ExecPipeline.willStall` can block a stage when pending enqueues exceed capacity or dequeues exceed size (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/executor/scala/ExecPipeline.scala:222-247`). However, streams are often allocated as default unbounded queues, file-read streams bypass `enq`, and LIFO pop is still not implemented (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/executor/scala/resolvers/MemoryResolver.scala:28-41`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/executor/scala/ExecPipeline.scala:179-180`). The top-level executor loops until `Done` without a timeout or deadlock exit, even though individual executors expose `isDeadlocked` (`/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/executor/scala/ExecutorPass.scala:45-67`; `/Users/david/Documents/David_code/spatial/src/spatial/executor/scala/ControlExecutor.scala:255-259`). A bounded Rust/HLS simulator must therefore define deterministic tie breaks, timeout policy, and wait-for diagnostics before tests can trust a deadlock.

## HLS-report-only Validation

HLS-report-only validation reduces simulator maintenance but misses pre-tool bugs and workload-specific failures. D-08 is clear that reports are the best post-tool authority for accepted II and latency, but not the hot-loop oracle; simulation is validation and triage, and policy rejects single-source extremes (`20 - Research Notes/50 - Decision Records/D-08.md:31-39`; `D-08.md:61-67`). D-21 adds that accepted II is unavailable before synthesis, reports may not map cleanly back to controllers, and queue/backpressure traces need the same II provenance (`20 - Research Notes/50 - Decision Records/D-21-research/06-risk-failure-modes.md:20-30`). Reports-only would catch performance/II mismatch after HLS, but it would not tell whether an elastic queue hid overflow, whether a bounded queue deadlocked, or which scheduler interleaving first exposed the failure.

## Dual-mode Semantics

Dual-mode semantics has the best risk profile and the highest ongoing cost. Keep `compat_scalagen_elastic` as the regression oracle for old ScalaGen value tests, and add `bounded_backpressure` as an opt-in HLS/liveness lane with explicit capacity, occupancy high-water marks, read-empty/write-full events, scheduler seed, timeout, and assumed II source. This mirrors D-15's warning that serial success must not bless HLS liveness and bounded failures should be provisional until D-22 is settled (`20 - Research Notes/50 - Decision Records/D-15-research/09-risk-failure-modes.md:27-33`). The cost is a larger test matrix and more labels: each result needs queue policy, scheduler policy, overflow/underflow policy, deadlock status, `spatial_compiler_ii`, `hls_requested_ii`, and `hls_accepted_ii`. That is still cheaper than letting one mode silently impersonate the other.
